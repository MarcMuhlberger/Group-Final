Integrating Your Cat
Integrating Your New Cat With Your Existing Pets
  1. Start by letting your cat loose in a small room (A bedroom works well where you are going to sleep). Give him/her time to adjust to this room.
  2. Make sure kitty litter box is really easy to find in the beginning.
  3. Keep other cats and dogs and loud children away for the first few days. Ideal isolation from an existing pet is 10 days.
  4. Introduce them slowly by switching rooms (letting existing pet into the room where the new cat was and vice versa). Smell is key for both pets.
  5. After about a week, or sooner if you are concerned, it is a good idea for the cat to see a vet near you.
  6. Average settling in time is one month. Please bear with them, they are frightened. Remember these cats are stressed from:
    • Being given up by their owner
    • Spending time in a loud noisy shelter
    • Travelling long distances to a new place
    • Spending time in a rescue
    • Seeing a vet on several occassions
    • Travelling again and being in a new place all over
