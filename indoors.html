Keeping Your Cats Indoors
The risks of putting your cats outside.
Death by motor vehicles
Roughly 65% of all outdoor cat deaths.
Poisonings
Weedkillers, antifreeze and rodent poisons. A fatal mistake for a feline to find.
Predator Animals
Owls, hawks, vicious dogs, snakes, or even other cats.
And so many more...
Think of the microscopic parasites, and the diseases that can be picked up by using gardens as litter boxes. Cats, being natural predators, can pick up tapeworms, fleas and roundworms by hunting small prey such as mice, moles, chipmunks and birds. Finally, there are several fatal diseases out there - Feline Leukemia, for which there is a vaccine, yes, but 100% effectiveness is questioned and Feline Aids, for which there is no vaccine, and which is always fatal.

Siamese Rescue can provide you with additional research statistics and information about the dangers facing outdoor cats.
Remember, you are getting a cat that has already been rescued from some form of abuse, abandonment or human apathy.
They've been through enough.
Your Little Predator Animal
Dont forget your cat is also a little predator animal, which means they will hunt for their prey.
Cats are not amoungst the native species. It’s a domesticated species. Their native ecosystem is indoors, alongside their human counterparts. If cats go outside, they are an unnatural component to an ecosystem. They’re a predator that normally doesn’t exist in these environments.
If you care for your local bird populations, keep your cats indoors.
